As AI becomes increasingly capable of performing tasks once reserved for clinicians, the most important questions are shifting from algorithms to systems. This talk will examine how AI is changing the nature of clinical work, patient participation, and healthcare delivery, and why model accuracy alone tells us little about real-world impact. Drawing on experiences from Learning Health Systems, digital health implementation, and human-centered design, I will argue that evaluation must begin long before deployment and continue throughout the innovation lifecycle. Ultimately, the future of clinical NLP depends on moving beyond asking whether AI can perform a task and instead ask a more fundamental question: What kind of care does AI enable?
